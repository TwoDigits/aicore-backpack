## Description

NotebookLM is an AI research and note-taking tool developed by Google Labs, powered by Google Gemini. Originally introduced in May 2023 as "Project Tailwind," it evolved into a stable product in October 2024. The tool acts as a virtual research assistant that helps users interact with their documents, generate summaries, create podcast-style audio overviews, and visualize complex topics through mind maps.

Unlike general-purpose AI assistants, NotebookLM is grounded in your uploaded sources - it only references the documents you provide, reducing hallucinations and ensuring responses are traceable to specific content.

## Key Features

- **Multi-Source Support**: Upload PDFs, Google Docs, Google Slides, Google Sheets, Microsoft Word documents, websites, and YouTube videos
- **Audio Overviews**: Transform documents into conversational podcast-style discussions between two AI hosts; users can now "Join" the conversation interactively
- **Video Overviews**: Generate visual slide-style videos with AI narration, images, and diagrams (added 2025)
- **Interactive Mind Maps**: Navigate complex topics and explore connections visually
- **Deep Research**: Generate extensive reports on topics from your sources
- **Study Guides & Briefing Docs**: One-click generation of structured learning materials
- **Source Citations**: All responses link back to specific passages in your documents
- **Multi-Language Support**: 35+ languages with output language selector
- **Mobile Apps**: Native Android and iOS apps (May 2025)
- **Discover Sources**: Find related web sources to add to your notebooks

## Why It's Cool

For developers, NotebookLM is valuable for:

- **Learning New Codebases**: Upload documentation, architecture docs, and READMEs to get interactive Q&A about a project
- **Research Synthesis**: Combine API docs, blog posts, and papers into structured summaries
- **Documentation Review**: Turn verbose docs into digestible audio overviews for commute listening
- **Technical Writing**: Generate briefing documents from scattered sources
- **Privacy-First**: Your uploads and queries are not used to train models or reviewed by humans

The Audio Overviews feature is particularly unique - it creates engaging podcast-style content that makes consuming technical documentation surprisingly enjoyable.

## Getting Started

1. Visit [notebooklm.google](https://notebooklm.google)
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. Create a new notebook
4. Add sources:
   - Upload files (PDF, DOCX)
   - Paste URLs (websites, YouTube)
   - Connect Google Drive files
5. Start chatting with your sources or generate:
   - Audio Overview (podcast)
   - Study Guide
   - Briefing Doc
   - Mind Map

### NotebookLM Plus

For power users, NotebookLM Plus offers:
- 5x more audio summaries, notebooks, and sources per notebook
- Custom style and tone for AI responses
- Shared team notebooks with analytics
- Available via Google One AI Premium or Google Workspace
